About Boracay

Boracay is an island in the Philippines located about 315 kilometers south of Manila and 2 kilometers off the northwest tip of Panay Island in the Western Visayas region. It is composed of the barangays Manoc-Manoc, Balabag, and Yapak, which belong to the municipality of Malay, Aklan. The island is managed by the Philippine Tourism Authority in coordination with the Provincial Government of Aklan. Originally home to the Ati tribe, Boracay is part of Aklan Province, which became an independent province on April 25, 1956. In 1990, it was recognized by the BMW Tropical Beach Handbook as one of the best beaches in the world, and in 1996, it was named the world’s number one tropical beach by the British publication TV Quick.

Decades ago, Boracay was a secluded island known only to a few, until tourism began to develop in the 1970s. It is said that a foreign movie crew accidentally “discovered” the island, while others credit German traveler Jens Peters, whose book’s rave reviews drew attention to its beauty. During the 1980s, Boracay became popular among backpackers and gradually transformed into a cosmopolitan destination. Today, it stands as one of the major tourist attractions in the Philippines, known worldwide for its powdery white sand beaches, vibrant culture, and lively tourism scene.

What to discover

Curated picks around Boracay and nearby Aklan.

City

Boracay invites you to explore warmth, beauty, and adventure beyond compare — a tropical paradise where crystal-clear waters meet powdery white sands, and every sunset paints a new story in the heart of the Philippines.

Country

The Philippines invites you to explore warmth, beauty, and diversity beyond compare — a paradise of over 7,000 islands where vibrant culture meets breathtaking natural wonders, from ancient traditions to modern adventures.

Hotel

Shangri-La’s Boracay Resort and Spa welcomes you to a world of luxury and peace — where lush greenery meets the blue sea, and elegant villas, private beaches, and gentle ocean breezes create the perfect island getaway.

Mall

D’Mall Boracay invites you to wander through its lively lanes filled with local charm — a vibrant mix of cafés, boutiques, and beachside treasures where island culture meets modern leisure, capturing the true spirit of Boracay’s tropical lifestyle.

Mountain

Mount Luho invites you to rise above the island and witness Boracay from a breathtaking new perspective — where gentle breezes meet panoramic views, and the beauty of land and sea unfolds in perfect harmony beneath the endless sky.

Tourist spot

White Beach welcomes you with its powdery white sands and crystal-clear waters — a perfect blend of peace and tropical charm, where golden sunsets, gentle waves, and island rhythms create the ultimate paradise escape.

Local Cultures & Traditions

Immerse yourself in the rich cultural heritage of Boracay and Aklan

Ati-Atihan Festival

Experience the vibrant Ati-Atihan Festival in Kalibo, a colorful celebration of dance, music, and Filipino culture held every January.

Piña Weaving

Piña Weaving is a traditional Filipino craft that turns pineapple fibers into delicate, handwoven fabrics. In Boracay and nearby Aklan, it represents creativity and cultural pride — showcasing the artistry, patience, and heritage passed down through generations

Local Cuisine

Savor the taste of Boracay’s island flavors — from fresh seafood, oysters, and mussels to inasal, chori burger, calamansi muffins, and coco mama the famous dessert in the island — all reflecting the island’s rich culture and warm hospitality.
